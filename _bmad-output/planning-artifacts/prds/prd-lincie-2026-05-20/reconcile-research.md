# Reconcile: technical-typeface-pairing-research vs prd.md

## Coverage summary

The PRD captures the headline commitments of the typeface research well: Newsreader + Commit Mono as the chosen pair, Astro 6 Fonts API + Fontsource provider, latin subset, italic-on-demand, preload only Newsreader normal latin, metric-matched Georgia fallback via `size-adjust` / `ascent-override` / `descent-override`, SIL OFL 1.1 mention on the faces, `hanging-punctuation: first` as Safari-only progressive enhancement, and the explicit ban on IBM Plex Mono and Geist Mono. The gaps concentrated around four PRD-altitude commitments are: the `font-display: optional` loading descriptor, the OFL license-attribution surface in the colophon, the three named escape hatches / rollback paths, and the Tier 1 pre-staging of local fallback woff2 files. Performance budget framing differs slightly between the two documents; nothing in the PRD contradicts the research, but several research-level commitments are softer or absent in the PRD.

## Gaps (content in research missing or weakened in prd.md, at PRD-appropriate altitude)

- **`font-display: optional` is not committed in the PRD.** The research repeatedly names `font-display: optional` paired with the metric-matched fallback as the CLS-prevention contract (Technology Stack Analysis "Browser Feature Support" section; Performance Integration; Strategic Recommendations item 3 implicit). The PRD's FR-4 commits to the metric-matched fallback against Georgia and CLS = 0, but never names `font-display: optional` as the loading descriptor. This is a PRD-level loading-strategy commitment, not a config detail; without it, the architect could reasonably pick `swap` or `block` and still claim conformance with FR-4. Add to FR-4 consequences.
- **OFL 1.1 attribution in the colophon is not captured as a requirement.** Research §7 ("Accessibility, Licensing, and Long-Half-Life Considerations") explicitly states the colophon is the recommended OFL attribution surface, and Architecture / Distribution Layer notes "recommended attribution lands in the colophon section of DESIGN.md." The PRD's FR-15 lists `Set in Newsreader and Commit Mono.` and the year, but does not require the OFL 1.1 attribution language for the two faces. Tighten FR-15 to make the OFL attribution explicit.
- **The three named escape hatches / rollback paths are not in the risk register.** Research §"Rollback / Escape Hatches" enumerates: (1) Fontsource provider regression → swap to `fontProviders.local()`; (2) Newsreader trend saturation → swap to Source Serif 4; (3) Commit Mono needing continuous mono weight → swap to JetBrains Mono variable. The PRD §14 Open Risks references the saturation mitigation only via typeface choice and FR-12; the provider-regression and mono-variable paths are absent. These are PRD-level mitigation commitments (each is one config-block change and earns a register entry).
- **Pre-staging local fallback woff2 files at Tier 1 is not committed.** Research Implementation Roadmap step 3 commits to `src/assets/fonts/Newsreader-Variable.woff2` and `CommitMono-{400,500}.woff2` being checked into the repo at Tier 1 to make the `fontProviders.local()` rollback a 5-minute change. This is the operational mitigation for the highest-impact provider-regression risk. The PRD's typography pipeline (FR-4) and §6 MVP Scope do not name this commitment.
- **Performance budget altitude differs from the research.** Research §"Performance Integration" / §6 ("Performance, CLS Prevention, and Subset Strategy") frames `~200 KB total for serif + mono with Latin subsets` (fonts only). The PRD §10 Cross-Cutting NFRs reads `Total page weight < 200 KB for HTML + CSS + fonts on first paint` (page total). Not strictly a contradiction — the PRD budget is more aspirational — but the fonts-only budget anchor that the research justifies is lost, which makes the PRD budget harder to defend at architecture phase.
- **90-day post-launch trend re-evaluation is not captured.** Research Risk Register and Future Outlook both commit to a 90-day post-launch re-evaluation of Newsreader and Commit Mono trend exposure (Architectural Patterns risk register, row 1; §10 Future Outlook). The PRD §14 mentions Fail #8 mitigation but does not encode the 90-day check-in as a tracked obligation. Lower-priority, but a PRD-altitude commitment.

## Contradictions (if any)

- None material. The performance-budget framing difference noted above is a softening, not a contradiction.
