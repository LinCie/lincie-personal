# PRD Quality Review — lincie

## Overall verdict

This is a strong, taste-disciplined chain-top PRD. The Glossary-first structure with a stated discipline rule, the normative SM→FR/UJ cross-references, the Non-Goals/Out-of-Scope split, and the named design rules in §11.6 make the document source-extractable for the UX, architecture, and story workflows it's meant to feed. The substance bar is unusually high for a personal site: anti-references, counter-metrics, and brainstorm-derived risk mitigations are all earned rather than ornamental. The few real risks are concentrated in done-ness clarity — a small handful of FR consequences underconstrain what they assert (notably warm-chroma carry, the "names of people" selector, and the OKLCH values per paper-tone band), enough to slow `bmad-create-architecture` if not tightened before handoff.

## Decision-readiness — strong

Decisions land as decisions, not as smoothed considerations. §4.3 Out of Scope removes the manual light/dark toggle and names the trade-off ("if a recruiter prefers dark UI universally, we accept that"). §6.2 explicitly gates promoting the project index and writing archive in nav on a 3-piece content floor, naming the brainstorm failure being mitigated ("Reason: brainstorming Fail #10 mitigation"). Counter-metrics SM-C1 and SM-C2 ("Do not optimize for time-on-page", "Do not optimize for traffic volume") are present, which is rare and tells the validator the team has thought about what failure looks like. Trade-offs are surfaced rather than dodged: FR-4 commits to `font-display: optional` with rationale ("Fallback first paint stays on the metric-matched Georgia stack until the web font has loaded; no FOUT-driven swap mid-paint"); the drop-cap glossary entry rules out `initial-letter` with reason ("Chromium support remains uneven"). §14.3 carries rollback paths for both faces. Open Questions are genuinely open (e.g., Q2 Lenis vs ScrollTrigger.normalizeScroll). The single `[NOTE FOR PM]` in §6.2 sits at a real tension (Tier 4 bench items being "emotionally load-bearing for an obsessive perfectionist") rather than a safe checkpoint.

## Substance over theater — strong

Personas are load-bearing and minimal: two primaries plus a tertiary builder-as-user, each driving a distinct UJ that in turn drives FRs (UJ-3 → FR-13's "no design work required" consequence). No persona feels added for thoroughness. The Vision is unmistakably this project — "The Quiet Atelier with a Warm Trace", "It exhibits and reads", "the colophon stands in for every conventional contact and CTA surface" — it cannot be lifted into another PRD. NFRs are specific, not boilerplate: "Font budget < 200 KB total for Newsreader + Commit Mono (latin subsets)", "page total HTML + CSS + fonts on first paint targeted under ~250 KB", "Lighthouse Performance ≥ 95", "CLS = 0". The §11.5 anti-references list is a tell that taste was actually exercised; "the 2025 cream-paper editorial portfolio with IBM Plex Mono everywhere, sterile and identical to fifty others" is the kind of sentence furniture-PRDs cannot produce.

## Strategic coherence — strong

There is a stated thesis the PRD bets on. §11.1: "The site is the work, not a frame around it. Its purpose is to evidence quiet confidence and considered craft through the experience of using it, so that the right opportunities and collaborators self-select." Feature prioritization follows from this — Tier 1 foundation, Tier 2 signature, Tier 3 polish, Tier 4 bench under the One-In-One-Out rule (§6.2, §11.6) — not from "what's easy first." Success Metrics validate the thesis: SM-1 ("at least one inbound email from a recruiter or collaborator references something specific from a project page or essay (not a generic 'saw your portfolio')") is a thesis-validating metric, not an activity metric. SM-2 validates the maintainability sub-thesis (UJ-3). The "small high-fit funnel rather than a large indifferent one" framing in §1 is operationalized by SM-C1 and SM-C2.

## Done-ness clarity — adequate

Most FRs carry multiple testable consequences and the testability bar is set high in places (FR-12: "A user agent that scrolls forward only never sees footnotes (verifiable by Playwright script)"; FR-2: "TR label updates within 60s of a real-time minute change without a page reload"; FR-4: "`dist/_astro/fonts/` after `bun run build` contains Newsreader normal latin woff2..."). What downgrades this dimension is a small set of consequences that don't actually verify what their FR claims, plus underspecified handoff details that downstream architecture and UX cannot resolve from the PRD alone.

### Findings
- **medium** FR-7 chroma carry not actually verified by its consequence (§4.3) — The FR asserts "Pure greys are forbidden; every neutral carries warm chroma." The testable consequence only checks "no `#` hex colors and no `rgb(` declarations for site tokens", which a zero-chroma OKLCH value (`oklch(0.95 0 95)`) would pass. *Fix:* add a consequence asserting a chroma floor (e.g., "every site-token color in `--paper`, `--ink`, `--meta`, `--hairline` has OKLCH `C ≥ 0.005`").
- **medium** FR-22 "names of people" selector left undefined (§4.11) — "Names of people: subtle variable-font weight increase (400 → 500) on hover" is testable in DevTools but the PRD never specifies how the site identifies a name. No class, data attribute, or markup convention is named. *Fix:* add to FR-22 (or Glossary) the selector contract — e.g., `<span class="name">` or `<cite>` — so UX/architecture can plan content authoring and so the third consequence ("A name's hover state increments computed `font-weight` from 400 to 500") has a definite scope.
- **medium** FR-8 paper-tone band OKLCH values not in PRD (§4.3) — FR-8 commits to four bands and asserts WCAG 2.2 AA contrast at each, but the actual `(L, C, h)` tuples per band are deferred to "Verified at implementation." DESIGN.md is the canonical home, which is fine, but `bmad-create-architecture` will need the values pinned to plan tokenization and contrast verification. *Fix:* either inline the four `--paper` and `--ink` tuples in FR-8's consequences or add a pointer like "see DESIGN.md §<heading> for OKLCH values per band" so the cross-ref resolves cleanly.
- **low** FR-3 "standard CRP budget" is an adjective (§4.1) — "Lighthouse Largest Contentful Paint includes Newsreader-rendered text within the standard CRP budget" — "standard" is unbounded. NFRs §10 say Lighthouse Performance ≥ 95 but no LCP threshold is named. *Fix:* either replace with an LCP millisecond bound (e.g., "LCP ≤ 2.5s on a 4G profile") or strike the consequence as redundant with FR-4's `font-display: optional` claim.
- **low** FR-20 quantification edge (§4.10) — "filter: blur(6-8px)" is a range; the consequence "renders with `filter: blur(6-8px)`" is fine for a designer but a Playwright assertion needs a single value. *Fix:* pick `7px` (or similar) as the canonical value and let CSS hold the truth.

## Scope honesty — strong

Omissions are loud, not inferred. §5 Non-Goals enumerates 16 explicit bans (analytics, newsletter, comments, CMS, light/dark toggle, i18n, OG theater, project gallery grid, studio framing, icon library, cookie consent, service worker, SPA framework, third-party fonts, separate token package, alternate animation libraries, runtime image manipulation). §6.2 Out of Scope for MVP separates "deferred" from "banned" with reasons attached to each item. The four `[ASSUMPTION]` tags are inline at the point of decision and roundtrip cleanly through the §9 Assumptions Index (verified: §4.3 FR-8 ✓, §4.8 FR-15 ✓, §4.9 FR-17 ✓, §4.12 FR-23 ✓). The seven §8 Open Questions are real and unresolved. §15 Acceptance gates advancement on assumption confirmation and Open Questions resolution, which closes the loop. Open-items density is calibrated to stakes — a personal site can carry seven open questions to design without being blocked.

## Downstream usability — strong

This dimension is what the PRD optimizes for, and it shows.

The §3 Glossary opens with a discipline rule ("Introducing a synonym anywhere in this PRD is a discipline violation") and is normatively referenced by the §11.6 Named Design Rules list, which is itself a UX/architecture handoff move. FR IDs are contiguous FR-1 through FR-25, no gaps, no duplicates. UJ-1..UJ-3 and SM-1..SM-4 / SM-C1..SM-C2 are likewise clean. Cross-references resolve: SM-1 cites FR-3, FR-11, FR-13; SM-2 cites FR-13 and UJ-3; SM-3 cites FR-3, FR-4, FR-7, FR-8, FR-17, FR-23; SM-4 cites FR-4 — every target exists. Each section is extractable alone via Glossary terms rather than "see above" prose. §13 Platform pins Astro 6 static, Vercel, Bun, Node ≥22.12.0, browser matrix — architecture can source-extract immediately. The "this PRD points; it does not restate" rule in §0 is honored throughout, with explicit upstream pointers (PRODUCT.md, DESIGN.md, brainstorming session, typeface research).

### Findings
- **low** UJ persona linkage uses ad-hoc names rather than §2.1 labels — UJ-1 introduces "Aiya the recruiter" and UJ-2 "Riza the collaborator"; §2.1 labels are "Primary: recruiters and hiring managers" and "Primary: potential collaborators." The intent is unmistakable and the persona type is named alongside the proper name, so this is not a blocker. *Fix:* on each UJ's first line add the §2.1 label in parentheses, e.g., "UJ-1. Aiya (Primary: recruiters and hiring managers) scans on a phone..." — costs nothing and makes the linkage machine-readable.

## Shape fit — strong

Hobby/solo personal site shape but with chain-top obligations into UX → architecture → stories, plus perfectionist launch-quality stakes. The PRD calibrates well: UJ density and persona rigor are higher than a typical hobby site but earned by the downstream chain. The §14.1 strategic risk register (5 risks, each linked to a brainstorm Fail# and a mitigating FR or scope rule) is heavy for a personal site, but reads as discipline rather than ceremony. §14.3 typography rollback paths sit at exactly the right altitude — pinned to the upstream typeface research, not duplicating it. No section feels like it was forced in by template; conversely, none feels conspicuously absent. The brownfield-vs-greenfield axis is a non-issue (greenfield), and no UJ pretends a single-operator workflow is multi-stakeholder.

### Findings
- **low** §6.2 Tier 4 bench list contains an unmigrated brainstorm fragment — "strict no-buttons rule [H5: hard ban; soft rule applies until adopted]" reads as a parenthetical from the brainstorming session that wasn't fully translated for a downstream reader. The §11.6 No-Buttons Rule already states this cleanly. *Fix:* drop the bracketed "[H5: hard ban; soft rule applies until adopted]" annotation, leaving "strict no-buttons rule" alone, or replace with "[see §11.6 No-Buttons Rule]".
