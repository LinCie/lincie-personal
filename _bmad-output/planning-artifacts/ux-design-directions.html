Surface 3 — Essay Page (with Drop Cap)

On Considered Interfaces

There is a particular quality to software that rewards re-reading. Not complexity for its own sake, but depth that reveals itself gradually, the way a well-edited essay yields new meaning on the second pass.[1] The best interfaces share this quality. They are simple on first encounter and rich on return.

Most portfolios optimize for the first impression. They announce, they summarize, they sell. But the visitor who matters most is not the one who bounces in three seconds. It is the one who stays for thirty minutes and leaves thinking about what they read.

This is the tension at the heart of any personal site: how do you serve the fast scanner and the slow reader with the same surface?
